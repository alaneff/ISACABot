# GRC (Governance, Risk & Compliance) Career Path

## Overview

Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) is one of the fastest-growing disciplines in cybersecurity and information technology. GRC professionals design, implement, and manage the programs that keep organizations compliant with laws and regulations, aligned to industry frameworks, and resilient against operational and cyber risk. Unlike pure technical security roles, GRC sits at the intersection of policy, law, technology, and business strategy — making it well-suited to professionals with both technical and analytical backgrounds.

In San Diego, GRC demand is particularly strong because of the region's dominant defense and government contracting sector. Companies working on DoD contracts must comply with CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification), NIST SP 800-171, and DFARS requirements — all of which require dedicated GRC expertise. Add in biotech (FDA compliance, 21 CFR Part 11), healthcare (HIPAA), and financial services (SOX, PCI-DSS), and San Diego offers one of the most diverse and robust GRC hiring markets in the western United States.

---

## Entry Level (0-3 years)

**Titles:** GRC Analyst, Compliance Analyst, Risk Analyst, IT Compliance Specialist

Entry-level GRC analysts typically support the implementation and maintenance of compliance programs. Day-to-day work involves:
- Conducting control assessments against frameworks (NIST CSF, ISO 27001, SOC 2)
- Maintaining the risk register and tracking remediation activities
- Assisting with vendor/third-party risk assessments (questionnaires, evidence review)
- Supporting audit evidence collection for external auditors or assessors
- Policy documentation and gap analysis support

Educational backgrounds vary widely: information systems, cybersecurity, business, political science, or even law are all represented. Technical curiosity matters more than a specific degree at this level. CompTIA Security+ is a common starting credential. CISA or CRISC exams are often planned for the 2-3 year mark.

For San Diego defense contractors, even entry-level GRC roles may require a DoD security clearance (Secret or Top Secret), which significantly boosts compensation.

---

## Mid-Level (3-7 years)

**Titles:** Senior GRC Analyst, GRC Specialist, Senior Compliance Analyst, Risk Manager

Mid-level professionals own portions of the GRC program independently. They lead assessments, manage relationships with business unit owners, and are expected to translate complex regulatory requirements into actionable controls. Key activities include:
- Leading framework implementation projects (NIST CSF maturity assessments, ISO 27001 readiness, SOC 2 Type II preparation)
- Managing the full lifecycle of vendor risk: due diligence, ongoing monitoring, contract requirements
- Running policy review cycles and exception management programs
- Presenting risk findings to security leadership and business stakeholders

At this stage, CRISC (Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control) becomes the most impactful credential to pursue. CISM and CISA are also valuable. Professionals working in defense contexts often pursue CMMC Registered Practitioner (RP) or Certified Professional (CP) credentials.

---

## Senior Level (7-15 years)

**Titles:** GRC Manager, Director of GRC, VP of Risk & Compliance, Deputy CISO (Risk)

Senior GRC leaders build and run entire programs, manage teams, and interact regularly with the C-suite, board, and regulators. They are responsible for:
- Setting the organization's risk appetite and tolerance in collaboration with the CISO and CFO
- Owning relationships with external auditors, regulators, and certification bodies
- Driving continuous compliance monitoring using GRC platforms (ServiceNow GRC, Archer, OneTrust, Tugboat Logic)
- Defining the metrics and KRIs (Key Risk Indicators) reported to the board
- Mentoring and growing junior GRC talent

At the director level and above, the GRC leader often has dual reporting relationships — to the CISO and to Legal/General Counsel — which requires political savvy and strong executive communication skills.

## Frameworks to Know

Mastery of frameworks is the core technical competency in GRC. Practitioners should be able to speak fluently to:

- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0** — The most widely adopted framework in U.S. organizations; updated in 2024 to include a Govern function
- **NIST SP 800-53 / 800-171** — Federal and defense contractor requirements
- **ISO/IEC 27001:2022** — International standard for information security management systems
- **COBIT 2019** — IT governance framework, especially relevant in IT audit-adjacent GRC roles
- **SOC 2 (Trust Services Criteria)** — Critical for SaaS and cloud service providers
- **CMMC 2.0** — DoD contractor requirement; tiered model (Level 1-3) based on CUI sensitivity
- **HIPAA / HITECH** — Healthcare compliance, relevant across San Diego's large healthcare sector
- **PCI-DSS v4.0** — Payment card industry, relevant for retail and financial services

## Salary Ranges (San Diego / National)

San Diego GRC salaries are elevated relative to national medians due to defense sector demand, clearance premiums, and cost of living. Cleared positions (Secret/TS/SCI) typically add $10,000-$30,000 to base compensation.

| Level | San Diego Range | National Median |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| GRC Analyst (0-3 yr) | $65,000 – $85,000 | $58,000 – $78,000 |
| Senior Analyst (3-6 yr) | $90,000 – $120,000 | $82,000 – $110,000 |
| GRC Manager (6-10 yr) | $110,000 – $150,000 | $100,000 – $140,000 |
| Director (10-14 yr) | $160,000 – $230,000 | $145,000 – $200,000 |
| VP / Chief Risk Officer (14+ yr) | $230,000 – $380,000+ | $200,000 – $350,000+ |

Note: Total compensation figures (including bonus and equity) are materially higher at publicly traded companies and large defense primes.
