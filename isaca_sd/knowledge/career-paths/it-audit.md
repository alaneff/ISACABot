# IT Audit Career Path

## Overview

IT audit is one of the most structured and credential-driven career paths in the technology field. Practitioners assess the design and operating effectiveness of IT controls, evaluate risk, and provide assurance to boards, executive leadership, and regulators. The field sits at the intersection of accounting/finance discipline and deep technical knowledge, making it both rigorous and highly compensable. In San Diego, IT audit professionals work across defense contractors, biotech, financial services, healthcare, and technology companies, with strong demand driven by regulatory requirements such as SOX, HIPAA, FedRAMP, and CMMC.

The defining credential is the **CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor)**, issued by ISACA. Holding a CISA is often a hard requirement for mid-level and senior roles and strongly preferred even at entry level. Most practitioners also pursue CRISC (Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control) as a complementary credential once they reach the senior level.

---

## Entry Level (0-3 years)

**Titles:** IT Audit Staff, IT Audit Associate, IT Audit Analyst, Internal Audit Associate

Entry-level professionals typically begin in one of two places: a Big 4 or regional public accounting firm, or an in-house internal audit department. The Big 4 path (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) offers exposure to dozens of clients, fast skill development, and strong brand recognition on a resume. In-house roles at companies like Qualcomm, Leidos, or SAIC offer more stability and a narrower but deeper focus on one organization's environment.

Core activities at this stage include:
- Executing test procedures for SOX IT General Controls (ITGCs): change management, logical access, computer operations, and program development
- Walkthroughs with process owners
- Documenting evidence in workpapers
- Learning audit methodology and frameworks (COBIT, COSO)

New graduates typically hold a degree in accounting, information systems, or computer science. The CIA (Certified Internal Auditor) or Security+ can help candidates stand out before sitting for CISA.

---

## Mid-Level (3-7 years)

**Titles:** Senior IT Auditor, IT Audit Senior Associate, IT Internal Audit Senior

At this stage, professionals take ownership of audit engagements, supervise staff, and begin interfacing directly with process owners and management. SOX audit experience is highly valued — candidates who have led ITGC testing and remediation cycles command a premium. Those with Big 4 experience often move into industry at this level for a salary step-up and better work-life balance.

Key focus areas:
- Leading end-to-end audit projects from planning through reporting
- Drafting audit findings and management action plans
- Assessing IT risk across ERP platforms (SAP, Oracle, Workday)
- Familiarity with cloud environments (AWS, Azure, GCP) and their control implications
- Mentoring entry-level staff

CISA is expected and often required. CRISC may be pursued near the top of this band.

---

## Senior Level (7-12 years)

**Titles:** IT Audit Manager, IT Audit Senior Manager, Audit Manager

Managers oversee a portfolio of audits, manage teams of 3-8 auditors, and own relationships with senior business stakeholders. They contribute to the audit plan, present findings to the Audit Committee, and are accountable for quality across the function. At larger organizations, senior managers may specialize in a domain: cybersecurity auditing, cloud auditing, third-party/vendor risk, or data governance.

Defense-contractor-adjacent companies (Leidos, SAIC, General Atomics) often need audit managers who understand government compliance frameworks like CMMC, NIST SP 800-171, and FedRAMP in addition to traditional IT controls.

---

## Executive Level (12+ years)

**Titles:** Director of IT Audit, VP of Internal Audit, Chief Audit Executive (CAE)

Directors and CAEs own the organization's entire audit universe, set strategy, manage external auditor relationships, and report directly to the Audit Committee. At this level, business acumen and executive communication skills matter as much as technical knowledge. Many CAEs hold CPA credentials in addition to CISA.

---

## Key Certifications at Each Level

| Level | Primary | Secondary |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Entry (0-3 yr) | CISA (in progress) | CIA, CompTIA Security+ |
| Mid (3-7 yr) | CISA (required) | CRISC, CIA |
| Senior (7-12 yr) | CISA + CRISC | CISM, CGEIT |
| Executive (12+ yr) | CISA + CRISC + CGEIT | CPA, MBA |

## Salary Ranges (San Diego / National)

Salary data reflects 2025-2026 market conditions. San Diego commands a modest premium over national medians due to cost of living, defense sector density, and competition from tech employers.

| Level | San Diego Range | National Median |

| Senior Auditor (3-7 yr) | $90,000 – $120,000 | $80,000 – $110,000 |
| Manager (7-10 yr) | $120,000 – $160,000 | $110,000 – $145,000 |

## Day in the Life

A typical day for a mid-level IT auditor at a San Diego defense contractor might look like:

- **Morning:** Review workpapers submitted by a staff auditor on logical access controls for a classified system; provide feedback via audit management software (Auditboard, TeamMate+)
- **Mid-morning:** Walkthrough meeting with the IT security team to understand their privileged access management process; document the process narrative
- **Afternoon:** Draft the preliminary findings memo for an ERP change management audit; identify a control gap where emergency changes bypassed the approval workflow
- **Late afternoon:** CISA study session (20-30 minutes) or ISACA San Diego chapter meeting preparation

Most audit work is documentation-heavy and relationship-driven. Strong written communication skills are as important as technical knowledge.
