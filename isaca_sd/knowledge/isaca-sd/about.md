# ISACA San Diego Chapter

## About the Chapter

The ISACA San Diego Chapter is the local chapter of ISACA International, a global professional association and standards body serving IT audit, cybersecurity, governance, risk, and assurance professionals. Founded in 1969 as the EDP Auditors Association, ISACA has grown into one of the most influential professional organizations in technology, with over 170,000 members worldwide and more than 225 chapters across 188 countries.

The San Diego chapter — operating at isaca-sd.org — serves the greater San Diego region, including professionals working in Chula Vista, Carlsbad, El Cajon, Escondido, and the surrounding communities. The chapter's membership reflects San Diego's unique professional landscape: a dense concentration of defense and government contractors, a growing biotech and life sciences sector, major healthcare systems, telecommunications companies, and an active technology startup ecosystem.

ISACA San Diego is a volunteer-led nonprofit. Its leadership team consists of elected and appointed officers — including a President, VP Programs, VP Membership, Secretary, Treasurer, and committee chairs — all of whom are active IT audit, security, and risk professionals in the local market. This volunteer structure means chapter programming is driven by practitioners who understand exactly what their peers need.

## Membership Benefits

ISACA membership operates at two levels: global (ISACA International) and local chapter. Members who join through or affiliate with the San Diego chapter receive benefits from both.

**Global ISACA membership includes:**
- Access to the ISACA online resource library: frameworks, audit programs, research publications, and case studies
- Discounted exam registration fees for CISA, CISM, CRISC, CGEIT, and CDPSE
- Access to ISACA's online community and job board
- Digital access to the ISACA Journal and industry research
- Complimentary or discounted access to ISACA webinars and online CPE courses

**San Diego chapter membership adds:**
- Priority access to chapter events, which typically offer lower or no-cost registration for members
- Local networking with San Diego's IT audit and cybersecurity community
- Study group participation for ISACA certification exams
- Career development programming: mentorship opportunities, career panels, and resume workshops
- Connections to local employers actively recruiting ISACA-credentialed professionals

Annual ISACA membership fees are modest relative to the value of access: professional membership typically runs $135-$145/year for ISACA International, with chapter dues of approximately $40-$75/year added for local chapter affiliation.

## Events and Programs

The San Diego chapter typically hosts monthly programming throughout the year, following an academic-year schedule (September through June) with lighter activity during summer months. Event formats include:

**Monthly Chapter Meetings:** The core chapter event, held approximately once per month. Meetings feature a guest speaker presenting on a topic relevant to IT audit, cybersecurity, GRC, or emerging technology risk. Speakers are drawn from local employers, consulting firms, regulatory agencies, and academia. Meetings include structured networking time before or after the presentation. CPE credits are awarded for attendance.

**Certification Study Groups:** ISACA San Diego organizes peer study groups for CISA, CISM, CRISC, and CDPSE candidates. Study groups meet regularly over a 10-14 week period, typically working through the ISACA official study materials chapter by chapter. These groups are among the chapter's highest-value programs for professionals actively pursuing certification — peer accountability and shared study significantly improve pass rates.

**Annual ISACA SD Conference or Symposium:** Many chapters organize an annual full-day or half-day professional development event featuring multiple speakers, panel discussions, and extended networking. This event typically offers the highest CPE credit yield of any chapter activity.

**Career and Mentorship Programs:** The chapter periodically runs career panels featuring established professionals discussing their career paths, advice for advancement, and how they use ISACA credentials in their work. Mentorship pairings connect senior members with early-career professionals.

**Networking and Social Events:** Informal networking events — happy hours, mixers — help members build relationships outside of formal programming contexts.

## Certifications and Education

ISACA credentials are the primary professional certifications in the IT audit, GRC, and security governance space. The San Diego chapter supports certification pursuits at every stage:

| Credential | Full Name | Primary Audience |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|
| CISA | Certified Information Systems Auditor | IT auditors, compliance professionals |
| CISM | Certified Information Security Manager | Security managers, CISO-track professionals |
| CRISC | Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control | Risk analysts, GRC professionals |
| CGEIT | Certified in the Governance of Enterprise IT | IT governance leaders, executives |
| CDPSE | Certified Data Privacy Solutions Engineer | Privacy professionals, data engineers |

All five credentials require passing a proctored exam and meeting experience requirements (typically 2-5 years in a relevant role). ISACA also offers continuing education requirements to maintain credentials (20 CPE hours per year for most certs).

The San Diego chapter's CPE program — spanning monthly meetings, study groups, annual events, and special workshops — is designed to help members meet annual CPE requirements through local participation.

## Local Cybersecurity Job Market

San Diego consistently ranks among the top U.S. metros for cybersecurity employment, driven by several reinforcing factors:

**Defense and Government Contracting:** San Diego is home to one of the nation's largest concentrations of DoD contractors. Companies like Leidos, SAIC, General Atomics, Booz Allen Hamilton, BAE Systems, and Cubic Corporation collectively employ thousands of IT audit, security, and risk professionals. Many roles require DoD security clearances (Secret, Top Secret, TS/SCI), which serve as a significant compensation premium and market differentiator. CMMC compliance requirements, now mandatory for DoD contractors handling Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), are driving substantial new hiring across the sector.

**Military Installations:** Naval Base San Diego, Marine Corps Base Camp Pendleton, Naval Air Station North Island, and several specialized commands (NAVWAR/PEO C4I) are major employers of civilian cybersecurity professionals and contractors.

**Biotech and Life Sciences:** San Diego is one of the nation's premier biotech hubs. Illumina, Thermo Fisher Scientific, Dexcom, Vividion Therapeutics, and dozens of smaller firms employ GRC, privacy, and IT audit professionals focused on FDA compliance, 21 CFR Part 11 (electronic records), and ISO 27001.

**Healthcare:** UC San Diego Health, Scripps Health, Sharp HealthCare, and Rady Children's Hospital are major healthcare employers with active cybersecurity and compliance functions. HIPAA compliance, healthcare-specific incident response, and medical device security are growing specializations.

**Technology and Telecom:** Qualcomm's headquarters in San Diego anchors the technology sector. Verizon has significant federal operations presence. The startup ecosystem in areas like Sorrento Valley and UTC continues to generate demand for security generalists and GRC professionals.
