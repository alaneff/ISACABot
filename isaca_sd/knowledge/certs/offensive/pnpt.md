# TCM Security Practical Network Penetration Tester (PNPT)

## Overview
The Practical Network Penetration Tester (PNPT) is a hands-on penetration testing certification issued by TCM Security. Unlike traditional multiple-choice certifications, the PNPT requires candidates to complete a realistic 5-day penetration testing assessment against a target network, followed by the submission of a professional penetration test report. A passing report triggers a 15-minute debrief with TCM Security staff. The PNPT is rapidly gaining recognition in the cybersecurity community as a credible, affordable, and practical alternative to OSCP — particularly for those who want to demonstrate real-world pentest skills without the high cost of Offensive Security's programs.

## Who Should Get This
- Entry-to-mid level aspiring penetration testers who want hands-on certification
- Security professionals who have completed foundational certs (Security+, eJPT) and are ready to demonstrate practical skills
- Those who cannot afford OSCP ($1,499+) but want a respected practical pentest credential
- Career changers from IT operations or sysadmin roles moving into offensive security
- Bug bounty hunters seeking to validate their methodology with a formal credential
- Those using PNPT as a stepping stone toward OSCP or GPEN
- Self-taught security practitioners who want a certificate they can show employers

## Requirements
- No formal prerequisites — the PNPT is designed to be accessible
- TCM Security strongly recommends completing their "Practical Ethical Hacking" course (Heath Adams / "The Cyber Mentor") before attempting the exam
- The exam attempt includes access to the exam environment; additional retakes are available at a reduced fee
- No renewal currently required — the PNPT does not expire once earned

## Exam Details
- **Format:** 5-day hands-on penetration test of a realistic Active Directory lab environment (multiple machines, internal network, external foothold)
- **Duration:** 5 days to complete the penetration test; 2 additional days to write and submit the professional pentest report
- **Deliverable:** A professional penetration test report (executive summary + technical findings + remediation recommendations) submitted via PDF
- **Debrief:** A 15-minute live video debrief with TCM Security staff after a passing report submission
- **Passing criteria:** Report must demonstrate successful compromise of the environment with professional, actionable documentation
- **Fee:** Approximately $399 (includes course access and one exam attempt); retakes approximately $200
- **Renewal:** Not required — lifetime credential as of current policy
- **Testing:** Self-hosted in TCM Security's exam environment, remote access via VPN

## Exam Domains / Skills Tested
- External reconnaissance and OSINT
- External network scanning and enumeration
- Exploitation of external-facing services
- Internal network enumeration post-foothold
- Active Directory attacks — password spraying, Kerberoasting, AS-REP roasting, Pass-the-Hash, Pass-the-Ticket
- Lateral movement and privilege escalation (Windows and Linux)
- Domain compromise and post-exploitation
- Professional penetration test report writing — executive summary, risk ratings, technical steps, remediation guidance

## Study Tips
- **Timeline:** Most candidates prepare for 3–6 months before attempting; those completing TCM's full course catalog can be ready sooner
- **TCM Security "Practical Ethical Hacking" course (Heath Adams / "The Cyber Mentor" on YouTube):** The core preparation course — available on TCM Security's platform or Udemy; covers the full methodology tested in the exam including Active Directory attacks extensively
- **TCM Security "Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) Fundamentals":** Covers the recon skills needed for the external portion
- **TCM Security "Linux Privilege Escalation" and "Windows Privilege Escalation" courses:** Essential for the post-exploitation phases
- **TryHackMe:** Complete AD-focused rooms and the "Jr Penetration Tester" path; AD exploitation is the hardest part of the exam for most candidates
- **Active Directory practice:** Set up your own lab with a Windows Server DC and a few joined machines using free evaluation licenses — practice the full attack chain from initial compromise to domain admin
- **Report writing matters:** The exam is not just about hacking — a poorly written report will fail even if you fully compromised the environment; study example pentest reports from GitHub and practice writing findings professionally
- **Heath Adams' YouTube channel ("The Cyber Mentor"):** Free walkthroughs of many relevant techniques; his teaching style closely mirrors the exam material

## Career Impact
- Qualifies for: Junior Penetration Tester, Ethical Hacker, Vulnerability Assessment Analyst, Entry-level Red Team Operator, Security Consultant (offensive, entry)
- Growing recognition among small and mid-market security consultancies, MSPs with security practices, and startups — particularly those that have discovered TCM Security's training content
- Not yet as universally recognized as OSCP or GPEN at large enterprise employers, but its reputation is growing rapidly year over year as more hiring managers have encountered it
- Average salary for PNPT-certified professionals: $65,000–$95,000 for entry-level roles; higher when combined with OSCP or experience
- Excellent launchpad for OSCP — candidates who pass PNPT find OSCP significantly more approachable; many use PNPT as deliberate OSCP preparation

## How It Compares
- **vs. OSCP (Offensive Security Certified Professional):** OSCP is the gold standard for penetration testing credentials and is more widely recognized by large enterprise employers and government contractors. It requires 24 hours of live exploitation plus a report and costs significantly more (~$1,499 for the course + exam). PNPT is more affordable (~$399), more accessible for beginners, and nearly as rigorous in testing practical skills — with the advantage of a 5-day window rather than 24 hours under extreme pressure. For those who can afford OSCP, pursue it; for those building skills and budget toward OSCP, PNPT is the best intermediate milestone.
- **vs. CompTIA PenTest+:** PenTest+ is multiple-choice (with performance-based questions) and covers broader content including engagement management and reporting concepts. PNPT is entirely hands-on and tests actual exploitation skill. PenTest+ is better for DoD compliance requirements; PNPT is better for demonstrating real-world offensive capability to technical interviewers. They serve different purposes and are not direct substitutes.
