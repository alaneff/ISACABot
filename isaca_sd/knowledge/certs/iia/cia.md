# Certified Internal Auditor (CIA)

## Overview
The Certified Internal Auditor (CIA) is the only globally recognized certification for internal auditors, issued by The Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA). It validates a professional's competency in the principles and practices of internal auditing across any industry or organizational type. Unlike CISA (which is IT-specific), the CIA covers all aspects of internal audit — financial, operational, compliance, governance, and risk management. It is the gold standard credential for internal audit professionals and is recognized by employers worldwide across public and private sectors.

## Who Should Get This
- Internal auditors at all career stages (entry, mid, senior) in any industry
- Financial auditors moving into internal audit roles
- Risk and compliance professionals who conduct or oversee audits
- Audit managers and Chief Audit Executives (CAE)
- Those working in governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) functions
- Professionals in finance, banking, healthcare, government, and manufacturing where operational audit is distinct from IT audit
- Those pairing with CISA for comprehensive coverage of both IT and operational audit

## Requirements
- A minimum of 24 months (2 years) of professional internal auditing experience or equivalent — this can be accumulated before or after passing the exams
- A bachelor's degree from an accredited institution (or equivalent); candidates with a master's degree may reduce the experience requirement to 12 months
- Completion of all three exam parts
- Compliance with IIA's Code of Ethics
- Membership in the IIA is not required but reduces exam fees significantly

## Exam Details
- **Three-Part Exam:**
  - Part 1: Essentials of Internal Auditing (125 questions, 2.5 hours)
  - Part 2: Practice of Internal Auditing (100 questions, 2 hours)
  - Part 3: Business Knowledge for Internal Auditing (100 questions, 2 hours)
- **Format:** Computer-based, multiple choice
- **Passing Score:** 600/800 for each part
- **Fee (approximate, USD):**
  - IIA Members: ~$365 per part (~$1,095 total)
  - Non-Members: ~$395–$500 per part (~$1,085–$1,500 total depending on affiliation)
  - Student/Academic members: reduced pricing available
- **Renewal:** Every 2 years via 40 Continuing Professional Education (CPE) credits
- **Testing:** Pearson VUE — in-person or online proctored

## Exam Domains

**Part 1 — Essentials of Internal Auditing:**
- Foundations of Internal Auditing
- Independence and Objectivity
- Proficiency and Due Professional Care
- Quality Assurance and Improvement Program
- Governance, Risk Management, and Control
- Fraud Risks

**Part 2 — Practice of Internal Auditing:**
- Managing the Internal Audit Activity
- Planning the Engagement
- Performing the Engagement
- Communicating Engagement Results
- Monitoring Engagement Outcomes

**Part 3 — Business Knowledge for Internal Auditing:**
- Business Acumen
- Information Security
- Information Technology
- Financial Management

## Study Tips
- **Timeline:** 12–18 months for all three parts; most candidates study 3–5 months per part
- **Gleim CIA Review:** The most widely recommended self-study system; comprehensive question bank, adaptive learning, and detailed explanations — strong alignment to actual exam questions
- **Wiley CIA Exam Review:** Strong textbook alternative to Gleim with thorough explanations
- **IIA Study Materials:** Official IIA prep materials are available but often supplemented with Gleim or Wiley for more practice questions
- **Take the parts in order:** Part 1 establishes the foundational concepts that Parts 2 and 3 build on; most candidates succeed better studying in sequence
- **Practice questions over reading:** The CIA exam is application-focused — doing 2,000+ practice questions will prepare you better than rereading textbook chapters
- **Join an IIA chapter:** ISACA San Diego members who are also IIA members benefit from study groups, networking, and local CPE events; IIA SD chapter is active

## Career Impact
- Qualifies for: Internal Audit Manager, Senior Internal Auditor, Director of Internal Audit, Chief Audit Executive (CAE), Compliance Manager, Risk Manager, GRC Analyst
- Recognized universally by public companies, financial institutions, government agencies, healthcare systems, and manufacturing — wherever audit functions exist
- The CIA is often required or strongly preferred for internal audit leadership roles; many CAE job postings list CIA as required
- Complements CISA for IT audit professionals — CIA covers operational audit rigor while CISA covers IT-specific audit knowledge; professionals with both are highly competitive
- Average salary for CIA-certified professionals: $85,000–$145,000 depending on level, industry, and location; CAEs with CIA can exceed $200,000 at large organizations

## How It Compares
- **vs. CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor):** CISA is an ISACA certification focused specifically on IS/IT audit, control, and governance. CIA is a broader internal audit credential covering all audit disciplines. Professionals who do IT audit specifically often hold both — CIA for audit methodology credibility and CISA for IT-specific knowledge. CIA is more recognized in finance-driven audit functions; CISA is more recognized in technology-focused audit and compliance roles.
- **vs. CPA (Certified Public Accountant):** CPA is an accounting and financial reporting credential primarily used in external (public) auditing and accounting practice. CIA is specifically designed for internal auditors. Many internal audit leaders hold both; CPA is better for public accounting and financial statement audit, CIA is better for operational and internal audit career paths.
