# SSCP — Systems Security Certified Practitioner

## Overview

The SSCP is an ISC2 credential designed for hands-on IT and security practitioners who implement and manage security controls day-to-day. Where the CISSP targets senior managers and architects, the SSCP targets the people who operate systems, apply policies, and respond to incidents on the ground. It is vendor-neutral, globally recognized, and significantly underutilized given the credibility ISC2 brings to a credential.

The exam consists of 125 questions to be completed within 180 minutes. The passing score is 700 out of 1000 points. The exam fee is $249 USD, making it a cost-effective entry point into the ISC2 certification family.

## Who Should Get This

SSCP is best suited for system administrators, network administrators, junior security analysts, and security operations staff who want a recognized credential that validates their practical security skills. It is also an excellent choice for IT professionals transitioning into security roles who already have one year of hands-on experience. Candidates who want to eventually pursue CISSP will find that SSCP builds familiarity with ISC2 exam style and the security domain vocabulary.

## Requirements

One year of cumulative, paid, full-time work experience in one or more of the seven SSCP domains is required. A four-year degree in a cybersecurity-related field can substitute for the experience requirement entirely, which makes SSCP achievable for recent graduates.

Candidates who pass the exam without meeting the experience requirement become an Associate of ISC2 and have two years to acquire the qualifying experience. This path is shorter than the CISSP associate window, so candidates should plan accordingly.

Renewal requires 60 CPE credits over a three-year cycle and the annual AMF of $135.

## Exam Domains

The seven SSCP domains are:

1. Security Operations and Administration (16%)
2. Access Controls (15%)
3. Risk Identification, Monitoring, and Analysis (15%)
4. Incident Response and Recovery (14%)
5. Cryptography (10%)
6. Network and Communications Security (16%)
7. Systems and Application Security (14%)

## Study Tips

The SSCP requires technical depth more than management philosophy — unlike CISSP, the right answer here is often the technically correct one rather than the business-risk framing. Study all seven domains but pay particular attention to access controls and cryptography, which trip up many candidates.

Official ISC2 study guides are available and straightforward. Supplement with practice exams to build comfort with the question format. Most candidates with relevant work experience can prepare in 6–10 weeks with consistent study. Labs and hands-on work in your day job will reinforce a significant portion of the material naturally.

## Career Impact

SSCP validates practitioner-level security knowledge with the credibility of an ISC2 credential. It is often overlooked in job postings because many hiring managers are only aware of CISSP, but it stands out on a resume for roles in security operations, system administration with security responsibilities, and junior security analyst positions. Government contractors and federal agencies recognize ISC2 certifications broadly, so SSCP can open doors in those markets at the operational staff level.

For career pathing, SSCP typically positions candidates for security analyst, security administrator, and junior SOC roles. The domain overlap with CISSP means that SSCP holders have a meaningful head start when they are ready to pursue the higher credential.

## How It Compares

SSCP vs. CompTIA Security+: Both are entry-to-mid practitioner credentials. Security+ is more widely recognized and required by name in DoD 8570 contexts. SSCP carries ISC2's brand weight and is more operationally focused. If you are targeting federal/defense roles, Security+ is the baseline requirement; if you are in enterprise IT and want ISC2 on your resume without the CISSP experience bar, SSCP is the better choice. Many practitioners hold both.

SSCP vs. CISSP: SSCP is the logical stepping stone. Same certification body, lower bar, lower cost, one domain of qualifying experience instead of five years across two. If CISSP is the goal, SSCP is a smart intermediate milestone.
