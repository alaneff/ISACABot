# Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA)

## Overview
The Cisco CCNA (200-301) is the foundational networking certification from Cisco Systems, validating the ability to install, configure, operate, and troubleshoot medium-sized networks using Cisco equipment and technologies. It covers routing and switching, IP connectivity, network access, IP services, security fundamentals, and automation basics. The CCNA is one of the most recognized and in-demand networking certifications worldwide and serves as the primary entry point into the Cisco certification track.

## Who Should Get This
- Network technicians, administrators, and engineers early in their careers
- IT professionals who work with Cisco equipment daily
- Help desk and systems administrators moving into network engineering
- Those targeting network operations center (NOC) or network engineer roles
- Professionals who have already earned Network+ and want Cisco-specific depth
- Anyone aiming toward senior Cisco certifications (CCNP, CCIE)

## Requirements
- No formal prerequisites — Cisco recommends basic understanding of networking concepts before attempting
- A single exam covers everything (consolidated from the older two-exam format)
- Certification is valid for 3 years; renewable by passing one of several qualifying exams, taking Cisco Continuing Education courses, or passing CCNP-level or higher

## Exam Details
- **Exam Code:** 200-301 CCNA
- **Questions:** Approximately 100–120 questions (multiple choice, drag-and-drop, fill-in-the-blank, simulation-based)
- **Duration:** 120 minutes
- **Passing Score:** Approximately 825/1000 (Cisco does not publish the exact passing score)
- **Fee:** Approximately $330 USD
- **Renewal:** Every 3 years via Cisco Continuing Education credits, passing a Professional-level exam, or retaking the CCNA
- **Testing:** Pearson VUE — in-person or online proctored

## Exam Domains
- Network Fundamentals — 20%
- Network Access — 20%
- IP Connectivity — 25%
- IP Services — 10%
- Security Fundamentals — 15%
- Automation and Programmability — 10%

## Study Tips
- **Timeline:** 3–6 months with consistent daily study; some background in networking can reduce this to 2–3 months
- **Jeremy's IT Lab (YouTube/Anki):** Free, comprehensive CCNA video series — widely considered the best free resource available; paired Anki flashcard decks reinforce retention
- **Neil Anderson (Udemy):** "Cisco CCNA 200-301 Complete Course" — highly rated paid course with strong lab exercises
- **Boson ExSim-Max:** The gold standard for CCNA practice exams — detailed explanations and simulation questions closely mimic the real exam
- **Cisco Packet Tracer:** Free network simulation tool from Cisco — essential for hands-on practice with routing, VLANs, ACLs, OSPF, and more; download via Cisco NetAcad (free account)
- **Subnetting:** Absolute must — practice until you can subnet any address in under 30 seconds; use subnettingpractice.com or Jeremy's subnetting videos
- **Simulation questions:** These are the hardest part; practice configuring devices in Packet Tracer regularly so the CLI commands become muscle memory
- Read through all 200-301 exam topics on Cisco's website and ensure you can speak to each one

## Career Impact
- Qualifies for: Network Administrator, Network Technician, NOC Analyst, Network Support Engineer, Junior Network Engineer, IT Infrastructure Specialist
- One of the most commonly required certifications in networking job postings — particularly for roles in enterprise IT, MSPs, ISPs, and government contractors
- The CCNA is frequently listed as a preferred or required qualification in positions that involve Cisco infrastructure (which is most medium and large organizations)
- Average salary for CCNA-certified professionals: $65,000–$95,000 depending on role, location, and experience level
- Strong stepping stone to CCNP (Professional) in Enterprise, Security, Service Provider, or Data Center tracks

## How It Compares
- **vs. CompTIA Network+:** Network+ is vendor-neutral and broader in scope; CCNA is Cisco-specific and technically deeper on routing, switching, and Cisco IOS. Network+ is better for demonstrating general networking knowledge; CCNA is better if you work with Cisco equipment or want to pursue a networking engineering career. Many professionals hold both. If you're working in a Cisco-heavy shop, start with CCNA.
- **vs. Cisco CCNP:** CCNP is the next level up, requiring CCNA-level knowledge as a foundation. CCNP dives much deeper into a specific track (Enterprise, Security, Data Center, etc.) and is aimed at senior engineers and specialists. Earn CCNA first, gain experience, then pursue CCNP when ready for the next level of responsibility.
