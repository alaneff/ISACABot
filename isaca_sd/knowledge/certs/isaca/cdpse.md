# CDPSE — Certified Data Privacy Solutions Engineer

## Overview

The Certified Data Privacy Solutions Engineer (CDPSE) is ISACA's privacy-focused technical credential, launched in 2020. It is deliberately positioned to differentiate from policy-only privacy credentials like the IAPP's CIPP (Certified Information Privacy Professional). Where CIPP certifies understanding of privacy law and policy, CDPSE certifies the ability to *engineer and implement* privacy into technology systems — privacy by design in practice, not just in principle.

CDPSE emerged as a direct response to market demand driven by GDPR (2018), CCPA (2020), and a wave of subsequent state and national privacy laws that placed technical obligations on organizations — not just legal ones. Someone has to build the consent management platforms, implement data minimization in application architecture, design access controls for personal data, and execute data subject request workflows. CDPSE validates that a professional can do that work.

The exam consists of 120 questions answered over 3.5 hours (210 minutes). Scoring is 200–800 with a passing score of 450. Exam fees are $575 for ISACA members and $760 for non-members. The exam is available at PSI testing centers and via remote proctoring.

## Who Should Get This

CDPSE targets technically-oriented privacy professionals:

- Software engineers and application developers who build systems that process personal data and need to apply privacy by design principles
- Security engineers who are expanding into privacy infrastructure (data classification, DLP, access controls for PII/PHI)
- Privacy engineers and privacy technologists in organizations scaling their privacy programs
- IT architects who design systems that must comply with GDPR, CCPA, CPRA, HIPAA, or other data protection regimes
- GRC/compliance professionals with a technical background who want to bridge legal requirements and technical implementation
- Data engineers and database administrators working with regulated data (healthcare, finance, government)

In San Diego specifically, CDPSE has growing relevance in biotech and life sciences (where HIPAA and clinical trial data privacy intersect), defense contracting (CUI and controlled data handling), and government-adjacent tech companies navigating FedRAMP and CMMC privacy requirements.

CDPSE is *not* the right credential for pure privacy attorneys, privacy policy analysts, or compliance managers with no technical background — the IAPP CIPP/CIPM tracks are better suited for that audience.

## Requirements

**Experience:** 3 years of cumulative work experience performing tasks related to privacy governance, privacy architecture, or data lifecycle management — the three exam domains. No formal waivers exist (unlike CISA), but the 3-year requirement is consistent with CRISC and lower than CISA/CISM.

- Experience can span multiple employers and roles
- Must be in tasks directly related to privacy governance, architecture design, or data lifecycle management
- Part-time work may qualify on a prorated basis

**Exam:** Pass the CDPSE exam; no strict ordering requirement with experience accumulation.

**Maintenance (CPE):** 120 CPE hours per 3-year period, minimum 20 CPE per year. Annual ISACA maintenance fee applies.

## Exam Domains

The CDPSE exam covers 3 domains:

1. **Privacy Governance (34%)** — Privacy program governance structures; privacy strategy alignment with business; privacy risk management; regulatory landscape (GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, LGPD, PIPEDA, APPI, and others); privacy policies and standards; vendor and third-party privacy risk; privacy impact assessments (PIAs) and data protection impact assessments (DPIAs); privacy metrics and reporting
2. **Privacy Architecture (36%)** — The largest domain. Privacy by design and default; technology and system architecture for privacy; identity and access management for personal data; data flows and data mapping; privacy-enhancing technologies (PETs) including tokenization, pseudonymization, anonymization, differential privacy; consent management architecture; API design for privacy; cloud privacy architecture
3. **Data Lifecycle (30%)** — Data classification and categorization; data minimization and purpose limitation; data retention and deletion policies; data subject rights implementation (access, portability, erasure, rectification); breach notification processes; records of processing activities (RoPA); cross-border data transfer mechanisms (SCCs, BCRs, adequacy decisions)

The technical depth of Domain 2 (Privacy Architecture) is what distinguishes CDPSE from other privacy credentials. Questions in this domain require understanding of actual technical implementation approaches, not just policy descriptions.

## Study Tips

- **ISACA QAE Database for CDPSE** — Smaller question pool than CISA/CISM but essential for understanding the question format and ISACA's perspective on correct privacy engineering decisions.
- **ISACA CDPSE Review Manual** — The primary study resource. More technical than CISM or CRISC study materials; read with attention to the implementation detail in Domain 2.
- **GDPR and CCPA/CPRA text familiarity** — You do not need to be a lawyer, but you need to understand the key obligations: data subject rights, lawful bases for processing, data breach notification timelines (72 hours under GDPR), DPIAs, and data transfer mechanisms. The exam tests applied understanding, not legal memorization.
- **Privacy by Design (Cavoukian's 7 Principles)** — Study and internalize these. They are foundational to how ISACA frames correct privacy engineering decisions throughout the exam.
- **NIST Privacy Framework** — Understanding this framework (Identify-Govern-Control-Communicate-Protect) helps structure Domain 1 content and aligns well with ISACA's framework-centric approach.
- Technical candidates (developers, security engineers) will find Domain 2 intuitive but may need deliberate study of regulatory content in Domain 1. Policy-background candidates will find the opposite. Identify your gap early and weight study time accordingly.
- Budget 60–100 hours of study time for candidates with active privacy or data governance experience.

## Career Impact

CDPSE is a relatively new credential (2020) but demand is growing rapidly as privacy regulation expands globally and organizations face engineering-level compliance requirements:

- **Privacy Engineer / Privacy Technologist** — Roles that rarely existed before 2018; CDPSE is one of the few credentials that directly validates the skill set
- **Security + Privacy hybrid roles** — As DLP, access governance, and data classification become privacy-critical, security teams are hiring engineers who understand both security controls and privacy obligations
- **GRC Engineer / Privacy Analyst (technical track)** — Bridges compliance requirements and system implementation; CDPSE + CRISC is a strong pairing for this track
- **Consulting** — Privacy engineering consulting is a fast-growing advisory practice at Big 4 and boutique firms; CDPSE differentiates consultants who can implement vs. advise

In San Diego's biotech and life sciences sector, the intersection of HIPAA, clinical trial data regulations (21 CFR Part 11), and California's CPRA creates significant demand for professionals who can engineer compliant data systems. CDPSE directly addresses this market gap. Defense and government contractors facing CMMC and CUI handling requirements also represent a growing market for privacy-capable technical professionals.

## How It Compares

| Cert | Focus | Audience | Technical Depth |
|------|-------|----------|----------------|
| CDPSE | Privacy engineering & governance | Engineers, architects | High |
| CIPP/E (IAPP) | European privacy law | Privacy lawyers, compliance | Low |
| CIPP/US (IAPP) | US privacy law | Privacy lawyers, compliance | Low |
| CIPM (IAPP) | Privacy program management | Privacy managers | Low-Moderate |
| CISSP (ISC2) | Broad security (includes privacy) | Security architects | High (security) |

**CDPSE vs. IAPP CIPP:** The IAPP CIPP credentials (CIPP/E for GDPR, CIPP/US for US law) are the dominant privacy law credentials. They are excellent for legal and compliance professionals but have minimal technical content. CDPSE complements rather than replaces CIPP for technical professionals — holding CDPSE + CIPP/E signals both engineering competency and regulatory knowledge, making a candidate particularly strong in enterprise privacy roles. For a pure technical audience, CDPSE alone is sufficient and more directly relevant than CIPP.
