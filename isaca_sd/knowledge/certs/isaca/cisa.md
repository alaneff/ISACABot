# CISA — Certified Information Systems Auditor

## Overview

The Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA) is ISACA's flagship credential and one of the most recognized certifications in IT audit, assurance, and control worldwide. First awarded in 1978, CISA validates a professional's ability to assess vulnerabilities, report on compliance, and institute controls within an enterprise. It is consistently ranked among the highest-paying IT certifications globally and is a de facto requirement for senior IT audit roles at public accounting firms, financial institutions, and government agencies.

The exam consists of 150 questions answered over 240 minutes (4 hours). Scoring is on a 200–800 scale, with a passing score of 450. Exam fees are $575 for ISACA members and $760 for non-members. Exams are delivered through PSI testing centers or remotely proctored online.

## Who Should Get This

CISA is best suited for:

- IT auditors (staff through senior) at Big 4 or regional public accounting firms
- Internal auditors at corporations, banks, or government agencies who cover IT risk
- IT compliance and controls professionals (SOX, HIPAA, PCI-DSS)
- Information security professionals transitioning into an audit or assurance function
- Risk and governance professionals who need to demonstrate audit credibility

It is not a good fit for pure hands-on technical roles (penetration testing, security engineering) — those are better served by OSCP, CISSP, or CSX-P.

## Requirements

**Experience:** 5 years of professional work experience in information systems auditing, control, or security. Up to 3 years can be waived through:
- A bachelor's or master's degree (waives up to 2 years)
- Certain other certifications (e.g., CISM, CRISC waive 1 year each)
- Full-time university instructor experience in IS/IT (1 year per year taught, up to 1 year)

**Passing the exam:** Required before or after accumulating experience. Exam results are valid for 5 years.

**Application:** Submit a completed application within 5 years of passing the exam.

**Maintenance (CPE):** 120 CPE hours every 3-year reporting period, with a minimum of 20 CPE per year. Annual maintenance fee applies ($45 member / $85 non-member as of 2025).

## Exam Domains

The CISA exam is organized into 5 domains (2019 job practice, still current as of 2025):

1. **Information System Auditing Process (21%)** — Audit planning, risk-based audit approaches, evidence gathering, reporting findings
2. **Governance and Management of IT (17%)** — IT governance frameworks, IT strategy, policies, performance management, COBIT
3. **Information Systems Acquisition, Development, and Implementation (12%)** — SDLC, project management, change management, testing controls
4. **Information Systems Operations and Business Resilience (23%)** — IT operations, infrastructure, incident response, BCP/DR
5. **Protection of Information Assets (27%)** — Logical and physical access controls, network security, data classification, cryptography, privacy

Domain 5 carries the most weight and tends to be where candidates lose the most points if they have a pure audit background without security exposure.

## Study Tips

- **ISACA Question, Answer and Explanation (QAE) Database** — The official practice question bank. Non-negotiable. Do at least 1,000 practice questions before sitting. ($199 member / $249 non-member for 12 months)
- **Hemang Doshi on YouTube** — Free, high-quality domain walkthroughs. Excellent for Domain 1 (audit process) and Domain 2 (governance). His mnemonics are widely praised in the CISA community.
- **Wiley CISA Study Guide** — Thorough textbook coverage. Better as a reference than a primary study tool. Useful for candidates who need conceptual grounding.
- **ISACA CISA Review Manual** — Official manual. Dense but authoritative. Pair with QAE rather than reading cover-to-cover.
- Study tip: CISA questions are scenario-based and test *best auditor judgment*, not just technical knowledge. When two answers seem right, choose the one that reflects proper audit methodology (e.g., plan before execute, verify before conclude).
- Allocate 90–120 hours of study time for candidates with 2–3 years of IT audit experience.

## Career Impact

CISA is a career accelerator specifically within the IT audit and GRC track:

- **Staff Auditor** → **Senior IT Auditor** (CISA often required at this level at Big 4)
- **Senior IT Auditor** → **IT Audit Manager** (CISA + experience is standard)
- **IT Audit Manager** → **Director/VP of IT Audit** or transition to CISO path

Big 4 firms (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) treat CISA as a near-mandatory credential for promotion into senior auditor or manager ranks on IT audit engagements. Government roles (GAO, IG offices, DoD) frequently list it in job postings. Salary premiums of $10,000–$25,000 annually have been consistently reported in ISACA's own salary surveys for certified vs. non-certified professionals in equivalent roles.

## How It Compares

| Cert | Focus | Audience | Difficulty |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| CISA | IT Audit & Assurance | Auditors, compliance | Moderate |
| CISM | Security Management | Security managers | Moderate |
| CRISC | IT Risk & Control | Risk/GRC analysts | Moderate |
| CISSP | Broad Security | Security architects | High |
| CIA (IIA) | Internal Audit (all) | Internal auditors | Moderate–High |

CISA vs. CIA (Certified Internal Auditor): CIA is broader and covers all internal audit, not just IT. For a career in IT audit specifically, CISA is the stronger signal. Many senior IT auditors hold both. CISA vs. CISM: These are complementary, not competing. CISA proves audit competency; CISM proves security management competency. Security-focused audit managers often pursue both.
