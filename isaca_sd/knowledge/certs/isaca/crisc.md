# CRISC — Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control

## Overview

The Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control (CRISC) is ISACA's credential focused specifically on IT risk identification, assessment, response, and the implementation of information systems controls. It sits at the intersection of IT risk management and enterprise risk management (ERM), making it a natural fit for governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) professionals who need to bridge the technical and business sides of risk.

CRISC was first offered in 2010 and has grown into one of ISACA's most popular certifications, frequently appearing on GRC analyst and risk manager job postings alongside or instead of CISA. The exam is considered slightly more approachable than CISA or CISM in terms of experience requirements, making it a realistic near-term goal for mid-career professionals.

The exam consists of 150 questions answered over 240 minutes. Scoring is 200–800 with a passing score of 450. Exam fees are $575 for ISACA members and $760 for non-members. Remote and in-person testing is available through PSI.

## Who Should Get This

CRISC is the right credential for:

- IT risk analysts and GRC analysts who assess and document risk across business units
- Internal auditors who want to emphasize the risk management side of their work (strong complement to CISA)
- Compliance professionals in regulated industries (finance, healthcare, defense) who need to demonstrate structured risk management capability
- IT professionals moving into risk roles from infrastructure, security, or operations backgrounds
- Third-party risk management (TPRM) and vendor risk analysts
- Security professionals who want to align their work to enterprise risk frameworks like COBIT or NIST RMF

It is *not* an entry-level cert. CRISC requires demonstrated risk management experience and is positioned for professionals who already understand IT controls in practice.

## Requirements

**Experience:** 3 years of cumulative work experience in IT risk management and IS control, across at least two of the four exam domains. This is a lower bar than CISA (5 years) or CISM (5 years with 3 in management), making CRISC accessible to professionals earlier in their GRC careers.

- The experience must be in risk identification, assessment, response/mitigation, or monitoring — not just general IT work
- No formal waivers exist (unlike CISA), but the 3-year bar and the domain flexibility make it achievable faster

**Exam:** Pass the CRISC exam within 5 years of application.

**Maintenance (CPE):** 120 CPE hours every 3-year period, minimum 20 CPE per year. Annual maintenance fee applies.

## Exam Domains

The CRISC exam was updated in 2022 and now covers 4 domains:

1. **Organizational Governance and Risk Management (26%)** — Enterprise risk management frameworks; governance structures; risk appetite and tolerance; aligning IT risk with business objectives; COBIT, NIST, ISO 31000 frameworks
2. **IT Risk Assessment (20%)** — Identifying IT risk scenarios; threat and vulnerability analysis; risk analysis methodologies (qualitative and quantitative); risk register management; asset classification
3. **Risk Response and Reporting (32%)** — The largest domain. Risk treatment options (accept, mitigate, transfer, avoid); control design and implementation; key risk indicators (KRIs); risk communication and reporting to stakeholders; third-party and vendor risk
4. **Information Technology and Security (22%)** — IT infrastructure; security architecture; change management; system resilience; cloud and emerging technology risk; data classification and privacy

COBIT is the dominant framework referenced throughout the exam. Familiarity with COBIT 2019 concepts will significantly improve performance, particularly in Domain 1.

## Study Tips

- **ISACA QAE Database** — Same recommendation as CISA and CISM: mandatory. The questions test scenario judgment, not memorized facts. Pay close attention to risk treatment option questions — the "best" choice is almost always "mitigate first, document residual risk" rather than avoidance or immediate acceptance.
- **ISACA CRISC Review Manual** — Official reference. Useful for domains where you lack practical experience.
- **COBIT 2019 Foundation materials** — Free summary materials are available from ISACA. Even light familiarity with COBIT governance objectives and enablers will help Domain 1 significantly.
- **Hemang Doshi (YouTube)** — Also covers CRISC in addition to CISA. Good for domain overviews.
- Practical tip: If you already hold CISA, studying for CRISC will feel familiar — the frameworks and question style overlap substantially. Many dual-credential holders report CRISC is easier after CISA because of this shared vocabulary.
- Budget 60–100 study hours for candidates with active GRC or risk experience.

## Career Impact

CRISC opens and accelerates a GRC-focused career path:

- **GRC Analyst** → **Senior GRC Analyst / IT Risk Manager** (CRISC frequently listed as required or preferred)
- **IT Risk Manager** → **Director of IT Risk / Enterprise Risk Officer**
- **Audit professional** → GRC pivot (CISA + CRISC is a powerful combination for senior audit/risk hybrid roles)

In practice, CRISC holders are concentrated in financial services, healthcare, energy, and government contracting — industries with formal enterprise risk management programs. The cert is also increasingly appearing in third-party risk management job descriptions as vendor risk programs have matured across industries.

Salary data from ISACA's 2024 survey shows CRISC holders in North America averaging around $130,000–$145,000 annually, varying significantly by industry and role seniority.

## How It Compares

| Cert | Experience Req | Focus | GRC Relevance |
|------|---------------|-------|---------------|
| CRISC | 3 years | IT risk & control | Core |
| CISA | 5 years | IT audit & assurance | High |
| CISM | 5 years (3 mgmt) | Security management | Moderate |
| CGEIT | 5 years | IT governance (executive) | High |
| CISSP | 5 years | Broad security | Moderate |

**CRISC as a companion to CISA:** Many IT auditors pursue CRISC as a second credential because audit and risk are closely related functions. Together, CISA + CRISC covers both sides of the assurance/risk equation and makes a candidate highly competitive for senior roles in internal audit, enterprise risk, and GRC consulting. CRISC is generally easier to obtain after CISA due to overlapping content and a lower experience requirement. The COBIT alignment in CRISC also deepens governance knowledge that is useful for CGEIT if pursued later.
