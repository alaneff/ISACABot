# CGEIT — Certified in Governance of Enterprise IT

## Overview

The Certified in Governance of Enterprise IT (CGEIT) is ISACA's most senior and specialized credential, designed for executives and senior leaders who are responsible for the governance of enterprise IT at the strategic level. It is the least commonly held of ISACA's five major certifications, with a relatively small but influential global community of holders — predominantly CIOs, IT directors, board-level technology advisors, and senior governance consultants.

CGEIT is built around COBIT 2019, ISACA's flagship IT governance framework, and tests the ability to apply IT governance principles across an entire enterprise rather than within a single function or domain. The certification signals that its holder can align IT strategy with corporate objectives, manage enterprise-wide IT risk, and ensure that technology investments deliver measurable value to the organization.

The exam consists of 150 questions answered over 240 minutes. Scoring is on a 200–800 scale with a passing score of 450. Exam fees are $575 for ISACA members and $760 for non-members. Testing is available through PSI testing centers or remote proctoring.

## Who Should Get This

CGEIT is appropriate for a narrow, senior audience:

- **Chief Information Officers (CIOs)** and Deputy CIOs who want to formalize their governance expertise and signal commitment to structured IT governance principles
- **IT Directors and VPs** in organizations with mature governance programs (especially regulated industries: financial services, government, healthcare)
- **Board-level IT advisors and non-executive directors** who serve on audit or technology committees and need governance fluency
- **Senior IT governance consultants** at firms where CGEIT is a differentiator for enterprise advisory engagements
- **Enterprise architects** and IT strategy leads who operate at the intersection of business and technology at the C-suite level

CGEIT is explicitly *not* appropriate for early or mid-career professionals. The 5-year experience requirement and the strategic scope of the content assume deep organizational context that is difficult to develop without substantial leadership experience. Attempting CGEIT without genuine enterprise-level governance exposure results in a poor study experience and low pass probability.

## Requirements

**Experience:** 5 years of work experience in the governance of enterprise IT. This is interpreted broadly — it includes board advisory roles, executive IT leadership, enterprise risk oversight, IT strategy development, and portfolio/program governance. At least 1 year must be in an IT governance-specific role.

- The 5-year requirement cannot be waived or substituted — there are no educational waivers for CGEIT
- Part-time roles may qualify on a prorated basis

**Exam:** Pass the CGEIT exam within 5 years of application. The exam is offered multiple times per year at PSI testing centers globally.

**Maintenance (CPE):** 120 CPE hours per 3-year reporting cycle, minimum 20 per year. ISACA annual maintenance fee applies.

## Exam Domains

The CGEIT exam (updated 2019 job practice) covers 4 domains:

1. **Governance of Enterprise IT (40%)** — By far the largest domain. Enterprise IT governance frameworks (especially COBIT 2019); governance system components; stakeholder engagement; IT governance board structures; aligning IT governance with corporate governance; evaluation and direction of IT
2. **IT Resources (15%)** — IT resource management including people, applications, information, infrastructure, and services; sourcing strategies; workforce planning; vendor governance
3. **Benefits Realization (26%)** — Value delivery; portfolio, program, and project alignment; IT investment management; performance measurement and reporting; business case development; realizing value from IT-enabled change
4. **Risk Optimization (19%)** — Enterprise risk management integration; risk appetite at the governance level; board risk reporting; emerging technology risks; IT risk and opportunity alignment

Domain 1's 40% weighting makes COBIT 2019 knowledge foundational. Candidates without a working knowledge of COBIT's governance objectives, enablers, and cascading goals will struggle disproportionately.

## Study Tips

- **COBIT 2019 Framework documents** — ISACA makes COBIT materials available at low or no cost to members. The COBIT 2019 Design Guide and Implementation Guide are essential reading, not optional.
- **ISACA CGEIT Review Manual** — Official manual; the primary study resource. Unlike CISA or CISM, the third-party study material ecosystem for CGEIT is thin — do not rely on finding comprehensive alternatives.
- **ISACA QAE Database for CGEIT** — Available but the question pool is smaller than for CISA/CISM. Use it, but understand it may not be as exhaustive.
- The exam tests *applied governance judgment* at an enterprise scale. Questions frequently present situations where a CIO or board advisor must choose a governance response — the right answer almost always involves structured governance processes, stakeholder communication, and framework-aligned decision-making rather than tactical or technical action.
- Study time for CGEIT is typically shorter (50–80 hours) for qualified candidates because their professional experience provides substantial contextual grounding — but the COBIT content requires deliberate study regardless of experience level.

## Career Impact

CGEIT operates at the apex of IT governance careers. Its impact is less about opening new job categories and more about credentializing authority that a senior leader already holds:

- Strengthens credibility for CIOs presenting to boards or audit committees on IT governance maturity
- Differentiates governance consultants competing for enterprise advisory engagements
- Provides formal recognition of governance expertise for executive-level performance reviews, promotions, and board nominations
- Supports C-suite professionals in regulated industries who need to demonstrate structured governance competency to regulators or examiners

Because CGEIT holders are relatively rare (compared to the tens of thousands of CISA holders worldwide), the credential carries meaningful signal weight in executive-level circles — particularly in organizations that use COBIT as their governance framework.

## How It Compares

| Cert | Level | Primary Audience | COBIT Alignment |
|------|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| CGEIT | Executive | CIOs, VPs, board advisors | Primary |
| CISM | Management | Security managers, future CISOs | Moderate |
| CRISC | Mid-Senior | GRC / risk managers | Strong |
| CISA | Mid-Senior | IT auditors | Moderate |
| CSX-P | Practitioner | Hands-on cybersecurity | Low |

Among ISACA certifications, CGEIT is the most specialized and the most senior. It is not a stepping stone credential — it is a capstone. Professionals who hold CISA + CISM + CRISC and have moved into CIO or senior governance roles sometimes pursue CGEIT to complete an ISACA credential portfolio, but it should not be targeted until genuine executive-level IT governance experience exists. The credential's value is proportional to the seniority of the role — for a CIO it is meaningful; for a senior analyst it is premature.
