# GIAC Certified Incident Handler (GCIH)

## Overview
The GIAC Certified Incident Handler (GCIH) is an intermediate-level cybersecurity certification from GIAC (Global Information Assurance Certification) that validates the skills needed to detect, respond to, and resolve computer security incidents. It covers the full incident handling lifecycle — from identifying attack techniques and malicious activity to containing threats and recovering systems. GCIH holders understand how attackers operate and can apply that knowledge to defend and respond effectively. It is closely aligned with the SANS Institute FOR508 course, though course attendance is optional.

## Who Should Get This
- Incident responders and security analysts (SOC Tier 2 and above)
- Security operations center (SOC) team leads and managers
- System administrators and network administrators who handle security incidents
- Professionals moving into defensive security or threat response roles
- Blue team members who need a structured understanding of attacker techniques
- Anyone who needs to demonstrate incident handling skills for DoD or government contracts

## Requirements
- No formal prerequisites, but GIAC recommends solid networking and OS fundamentals (equivalent to Security+ or GCIH practice exam results)
- Significant hands-on experience in a security operations or system administration role is strongly advised
- Certification valid for 4 years; renewable via 36 CPE credits

## Exam Details
- **Questions:** 106 questions
- **Duration:** 4 hours
- **Format:** Open book (physical notes and printed materials allowed — no electronic devices during exam)
- **Passing Score:** 73%
- **Fee:** Approximately $959 for the exam attempt (includes one retake attempt if needed within 30 days)
- **Renewal:** Every 4 years via 36 CPE credits or retaking the exam
- **Testing:** Pearson VUE proctored (in-person or online via ProctorU)

## Exam Domains
- Incident Handling and Investigation — covers the full IR lifecycle
- Computer Crime Investigation and Ethics
- Attack Techniques — scanning, enumeration, exploitation, persistence
- Malicious Code — malware behavior, analysis basics
- Network Investigations — packet analysis, log analysis
- Hacker Exploits — web attacks, password attacks, session hijacking
- Denial of Service and Session Hijacking
- Windows and Linux exploit techniques

## Study Tips
- **Timeline:** 2–4 months if self-studying; shorter if attending SANS FOR508 ("Advanced Incident Response, Threat Hunting, and Digital Forensics")
- **SANS SEC504 course** ("Hacker Tools, Techniques, Exploits, and Incident Handling") is the primary aligned course for GCIH — far more directly relevant than FOR508 for the GCIH exam; SEC504 is the one to take if budgeting for a SANS course
- **Index your materials:** Because the exam is open book, the key to passing is building a well-organized index of your notes and printed materials. Speed of lookup matters enormously — 4 hours for 106 questions means you cannot read through your notes for every question
- **GIAC practice exams:** Take both included practice exams before your test date — they are realistic and help identify weak areas
- **Attack technique knowledge is critical:** Understand nmap scanning, Metasploit basics, password cracking (hashcat, John), web attacks (SQLi, XSS), lateral movement methods, and persistence mechanisms
- **Build a personal lab:** Practice setting up Kali Linux, running exploits against intentionally vulnerable VMs (Metasploitable, DVWA), and then responding to the resulting indicators — this hands-on experience is invaluable

## Career Impact
- Qualifies for: Incident Responder, SOC Analyst (Tier 2/3), Threat Analyst, Security Analyst, Cyber Defense Analyst, Digital Forensics Analyst (entry-level)
- Recognized by DoD under 8570/8140 for CSSP Incident Responder roles
- Strong recognition among financial institutions, healthcare organizations, defense contractors, and large enterprises with mature security operations
- Average salary for GCIH-certified professionals: $80,000–$115,000 depending on role, location, and clearance level
- Excellent credential for SOC professionals demonstrating they understand both attacker techniques and response methodology

## How It Compares
- **vs. CompTIA CySA+ (Cybersecurity Analyst):** CySA+ is a broader, less expensive certification covering threat and vulnerability management. GCIH is more prestigious in the incident response community and goes deeper into attack techniques. If budget allows, GCIH is more respected by security-mature employers; CySA+ is a good stepping stone if GCIH is not yet within reach.
- **vs. GIAC GCFE/GCFA (Forensics):** GCIH focuses on the response and handling side of incidents; GCFE/GCFA focus on forensic investigation after the fact. For SOC analysts focused on live response, GCIH is the right choice. For those doing deep forensic investigation or malware analysis, GCFA is more appropriate. Many professionals pursue both over time.
