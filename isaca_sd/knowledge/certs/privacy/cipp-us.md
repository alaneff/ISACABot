# IAPP Certified Information Privacy Professional/United States (CIPP/US)

## Overview
The CIPP/US (Certified Information Privacy Professional/United States) is the leading privacy certification for professionals working with U.S. privacy and data protection law. Issued by the International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP), it validates knowledge of the complex patchwork of U.S. federal and state privacy regulations — including CCPA/CPRA, HIPAA, FERPA, COPPA, GLBA, FCRA, and emerging state laws. It is the benchmark credential for privacy professionals operating in the U.S. legal environment and is often the first IAPP certification that North American-focused privacy practitioners earn.

## Who Should Get This
- Privacy lawyers and legal professionals advising on U.S. privacy compliance
- Compliance officers managing HIPAA, CCPA, or other U.S. regulatory requirements
- Healthcare and financial services professionals with data privacy responsibilities
- HR professionals managing employee data under U.S. law
- Marketing and product professionals building compliant data practices
- Privacy engineers and information security professionals expanding into privacy
- Consultants advising clients on U.S. privacy risk and compliance programs

## Requirements
- No formal prerequisites — the IAPP recommends professional experience in privacy, law, or a related field but the exam is open to anyone
- Passing a single exam earns the credential
- Certification valid for 2 years; renewable via 20 Continuing Privacy Education (CPE) credits
- IAPP membership provides access to discounted study materials and ongoing education opportunities

## Exam Details
- **Questions:** 90 questions (multiple choice)
- **Duration:** 2.5 hours (150 minutes)
- **Format:** Closed book, computer-based
- **Passing Score:** Approximately 300/400 (scaled scoring)
- **Fee:** Approximately $550 USD for the exam; same pricing as CIPP/E; IAPP membership (~$250/year) provides access to study resources
- **Renewal:** Every 2 years via 20 CPE credits
- **Testing:** IAPP testing centers or online via OnVUE (Pearson VUE)

## Exam Domains
- Introduction to U.S. Privacy Law — constitutional foundations, FTC authority, sectoral approach
- Healthcare Privacy — HIPAA Privacy Rule, Security Rule, HITECH, minimum necessary standard, enforcement
- Financial Privacy — GLBA (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act), FCRA (Fair Credit Reporting Act), FTC regulations
- Education Privacy — FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act), PPRA
- Children's Privacy — COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act), CARU guidelines
- State Privacy Laws — CCPA/CPRA (California), and major state privacy laws (Virginia, Colorado, Texas, etc.)
- Online Privacy — behavioral advertising, tracking, cookie consent, email laws (CAN-SPAM)
- Workplace Privacy — employee monitoring, background checks, NLRA considerations
- Enforcement and Litigation — FTC Section 5, state AG enforcement, private rights of action

## Study Tips
- **Timeline:** 6–10 weeks with consistent study for someone with some legal or compliance background; 10–14 weeks for those new to privacy law
- **IAPP Official Textbook:** "U.S. Private Sector Privacy" — the primary study reference; organized clearly by sector and regulation
- **IAPP Body of Knowledge:** Download the official exam blueprint from iapp.org and ensure you can address each bullet point
- **State law landscape is evolving:** Pay particular attention to CCPA/CPRA (California) and the growing number of state comprehensive privacy laws; this content has grown significantly in recent exam versions
- **IAPP Flashcards:** Available through membership; excellent for memorizing key regulatory thresholds and definitions
- **Key facts to memorize:** HIPAA's 45-day breach notification (60 days for large breaches), COPPA's age threshold (under 13), GLBA's opt-out model, CCPA's opt-out vs. opt-in distinctions, FERPA's consent requirements
- **Practice exams:** Official IAPP practice questions are the closest to the real exam; supplement with any available third-party question banks
- **Stay current:** U.S. privacy law is active — new state laws pass frequently; check IAPP's privacy law chart for current state coverage

## Career Impact
- Qualifies for: Privacy Counsel, Privacy Manager, Compliance Analyst, Data Privacy Officer (U.S. context), Chief Privacy Officer (CPO), HIPAA Privacy Officer, Privacy Consultant
- Recognized by healthcare organizations, financial institutions, tech companies, law firms, and consulting firms as the baseline U.S. privacy credential
- Frequently listed as preferred or required in privacy job postings that involve U.S. regulatory compliance, particularly roles involving HIPAA, CCPA, or multi-state compliance
- Average salary for CIPP/US-certified professionals: $85,000–$145,000 depending on role, industry, and seniority
- Strong credential to pair with CIPP/E for global privacy roles or with CIPM for privacy leadership positions

## How It Compares
- **vs. CIPP/E:** CIPP/E focuses on European data protection law (GDPR); CIPP/US focuses on the U.S. regulatory patchwork. They are frequently held together by privacy professionals working in global organizations or multinationals. If your work involves both EU data subjects and U.S. compliance, pursuing both credentials significantly broadens career options. Start with whichever jurisdiction is more relevant to your current role.
- **vs. CIPM (Certified Information Privacy Manager):** CIPP/US demonstrates jurisdiction-specific legal knowledge; CIPM demonstrates ability to design, build, and manage a privacy program. Many privacy leaders hold CIPP/US + CIPM to show both regulatory competency and operational program management skills. The IAPP refers to CIPP/US + CIPM as its core combination for privacy professionals in the U.S. market.
