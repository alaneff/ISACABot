# CompTIA Network+ (Network+)

## Overview
CompTIA Network+ is a vendor-neutral networking certification that validates the skills needed to design, configure, manage, and troubleshoot wired and wireless networks. Issued by CompTIA, it covers network infrastructure, operations, security basics, and troubleshooting. It is widely recognized across the industry as a solid baseline for networking professionals and is approved under the DoD 8570/8140 directive.

## Who Should Get This
- IT professionals with some A+-level foundation looking to specialize in networking
- Network administrators and support technicians early in their careers
- Systems administrators who need a formal networking credential
- Help desk professionals moving toward infrastructure roles
- Anyone targeting DoD IAT Level I or network-related government contract positions
- Those preparing for more advanced networking certs like CCNA or Security+

## Requirements
- No formal prerequisites — CompTIA recommends CompTIA A+ and 9–12 months of networking experience
- Single exam required (unlike A+'s two-exam format)
- Certification is valid for 3 years; renewable via CEUs or by passing a higher-level cert

## Exam Details
- **Exam Code:** N10-009 (current version as of 2024)
- **Questions:** Up to 90 questions (multiple choice, drag-and-drop, performance-based)
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Passing Score:** 720/900
- **Fee:** Approximately $369
- **Renewal:** Every 3 years via 30 CEUs or passing a qualifying higher-level CompTIA exam
- **Testing:** Pearson VUE — in-person or online proctored

## Exam Domains
- Networking Concepts — 23%
- Network Implementation — 19%
- Network Operations — 17%
- Network Security — 20%
- Network Troubleshooting — 21%

## Study Tips
- **Timeline:** 2–3 months for those with IT support background; 4–5 months for those new to networking
- **Professor Messer:** Free N10-009 video course at professormesser.com — the most recommended free resource; his study groups and practice tests are very effective
- **Mike Meyers (Total Seminars):** "CompTIA Network+ All-in-One Exam Guide" — extremely thorough textbook, great for conceptual understanding
- **Jason Dion:** Udemy video course and practice exam sets — highly rated for exam-style question practice
- **Hands-on lab work:** Use Cisco Packet Tracer (free) or GNS3 to practice subnetting, VLANs, routing protocols, and switching scenarios
- **Subnetting:** Master CIDR notation and subnet calculation — these show up heavily on the exam; use subnettingpractice.com
- **OSI Model:** Know every layer cold — nearly every scenario question maps back to it
- Study the N10-009 objectives document from CompTIA's website and make sure you can address every bullet point

## Career Impact
- Qualifies for: Network Technician, Network Administrator, Systems Administrator (entry), NOC Analyst, IT Infrastructure Support
- Approved for DoD 8570/8140 IAT Level I (for some role categories) and serves as a baseline for government and defense contractor positions
- Recognized by major employers including government contractors, MSPs, healthcare systems, and enterprises
- Average salary range: $55,000–$80,000 depending on role, location, and additional certifications
- Strong stepping stone toward CCNA (Cisco), Security+, or Cloud+ certifications

## How It Compares
- **vs. Cisco CCNA:** Network+ is vendor-neutral and broader; CCNA is Cisco-specific and more technically deep on routing, switching, and Cisco IOS. Network+ is better for general networking roles or as an entry credential; CCNA is better if you work primarily with Cisco gear or want to specialize as a network engineer. Many professionals earn both.
- **vs. CompTIA Security+:** Security+ focuses on cybersecurity and is the next step after Network+ for those going into security. Network+ should generally come first as it builds the infrastructure knowledge Security+ assumes you have.
