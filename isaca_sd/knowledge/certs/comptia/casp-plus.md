# CompTIA Advanced Security Practitioner (CASP+)

## Overview
CompTIA CASP+ (CAS-004) is an advanced-level cybersecurity certification for security practitioners who design and implement solutions rather than manage teams or programs. Issued by CompTIA, it is the highest-level technical security certification in the CompTIA portfolio. CASP+ proves mastery across enterprise security architecture, risk management, integration of computing disciplines, and research and collaboration in complex environments. Notably, CASP+ does not expire — it is perpetual once earned — though CompTIA offers an optional renewal path.

## Who Should Get This
- Senior security engineers and architects with 10+ years of IT experience (5+ in security)
- Security practitioners who want to stay technical rather than move into management
- Those who need an advanced security credential without the management focus of CISSP
- DoD contractors and government employees requiring IAT Level III or IASAE certification
- Experienced professionals in blue team, red team, or security architecture roles
- CISSP holders who want a technically-focused complement

## Requirements
- No formal prerequisites, but CompTIA recommends at least 10 years of IT experience, including 5 years of hands-on technical security experience
- Security+ or equivalent knowledge is assumed
- Single exam required
- CASP+ does not expire; it is a lifetime credential (unlike most CompTIA certs with 3-year cycles)

## Exam Details
- **Exam Code:** CAS-004
- **Questions:** Up to 90 questions (multiple choice and performance-based — no purely conceptual questions; all are scenario-driven)
- **Duration:** 165 minutes
- **Passing Score:** Pass/Fail (no numeric score published — CompTIA uses compensatory scoring)
- **Fee:** Approximately $494
- **Renewal:** Perpetual — does not require renewal; optional participation in CompTIA's CE program for ongoing learning credit
- **Testing:** Pearson VUE — in-person or online proctored

## Exam Domains
- Security Architecture — 29%
- Security Operations — 30%
- Security Engineering and Cryptography — 26%
- Governance, Risk, and Compliance — 15%

## Study Tips
- **Timeline:** 3–5 months for experienced security professionals; this exam assumes deep technical knowledge
- **CompTIA CASP+ Study Guide (Jeff Parker / Wm. Arthur Conklin):** The primary study guide — thorough coverage of all CAS-004 objectives
- **CompTIA CertMaster Learn:** Official platform with interactive content and labs; recommended if budget allows
- **CASP+ All-in-One (Nicholas Lane):** Alternative comprehensive text with good scenario coverage
- **Practice exams are critical:** CASP+ is scenario-heavy; practice reasoning through complex multi-step problems, not just memorizing facts
- **Review cryptography deeply:** Algorithms, key management, PKI, TLS — this section trips many experienced professionals
- **Architecture scenarios:** Practice evaluating trade-offs between security controls in enterprise environments — the exam tests judgment, not just recall
- The performance-based questions (PBQs) are the hardest part — allocate significant time to hands-on lab practice using tools like Wireshark, network analyzers, and scripting

## Career Impact
- Qualifies for: Senior Security Engineer, Security Architect, Principal Security Analyst, Senior Penetration Tester, Security Researcher, Chief Security Engineer
- Approved under DoD 8570/8140 for IAT Level III and IASAE (Information Assurance System Architecture and Engineering) Level I and II — one of the few certifications covering these levels without requiring management experience
- Recognized by defense contractors, federal agencies, and large enterprise employers
- Average salary for CASP+-certified professionals: $100,000–$145,000 depending on role, clearance, and location
- Valuable for practitioners who want CISSP-level recognition without taking on managerial responsibilities

## How It Compares
- **vs. CISSP:** CISSP is the gold standard for security management and leadership roles and has broader industry recognition. However, CISSP has a management and policy orientation; CASP+ is purely technical and hands-on. If you want to lead security programs or move into CISO-track roles, pursue CISSP. If you want to remain a technical practitioner and architect, CASP+ is the better fit. For DoD roles, CASP+ covers IAT Level III and IASAE levels that CISSP does not.
- **vs. GIAC GSTRT / GCIH:** GIAC certifications are generally more specialized; CASP+ is broader in covering enterprise architecture across multiple security domains. CASP+ is better if you need a single advanced generalist credential; GIAC certs are better for deep specialization in a specific area.
