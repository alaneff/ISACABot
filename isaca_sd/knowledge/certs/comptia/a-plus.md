# CompTIA A+ (A+)

## Overview
CompTIA A+ is the industry-standard entry-level IT certification covering hardware, software, operating systems, networking basics, and troubleshooting. Issued by CompTIA, it is vendor-neutral and validates the foundational skills needed to work as an IT support technician. It is one of the most widely recognized entry-level credentials globally and is often the first certification IT professionals earn.

## Who Should Get This
- Individuals entering IT for the first time with little or no professional experience
- Help desk, desktop support, and field technician candidates
- Career changers moving into technology roles
- IT support staff looking to formalize and validate existing skills
- Students completing IT or computer science programs
- Military veterans transitioning into civilian IT roles

## Requirements
- No formal prerequisites — CompTIA recommends 9–12 months of hands-on lab experience, but this is not enforced
- Must pass two separate exams: Core 1 (220-1101) and Core 2 (220-1102) — both must be passed to earn the certification
- Certification is valid for 3 years; renewal via continuing education (CEUs) or retaking the exam

## Exam Details
- **Exams:** 220-1101 (Core 1) and 220-1102 (Core 2) — both required
- **Questions:** Up to 90 questions per exam (multiple choice, drag-and-drop, performance-based)
- **Duration:** 90 minutes per exam
- **Passing Score:** 675/900 for Core 1; 700/900 for Core 2
- **Fee:** Approximately $246 per exam ($492 total for both); voucher bundles available
- **Renewal:** Every 3 years via 20 CEUs or passing a higher CompTIA exam (Network+, Security+, etc.)
- **Testing:** Pearson VUE — in-person or online proctored

## Exam Domains

**Core 1 (220-1101):**
- Mobile Devices — 15%
- Networking — 20%
- Hardware — 25%
- Virtualization and Cloud Computing — 11%
- Hardware and Network Troubleshooting — 29%

**Core 2 (220-1102):**
- Operating Systems — 31%
- Security — 25%
- Software Troubleshooting — 22%
- Operational Procedures — 22%

## Study Tips
- **Timeline:** 2–4 months for those with some hands-on experience; 4–6 months for complete beginners
- **Professor Messer:** Free video series on professormesser.com — comprehensive and closely aligned to exam objectives; his practice tests are excellent
- **CompTIA CertMaster:** Official online learning platform with labs and practice exams (paid, but thorough)
- **Mike Meyers (Total Seminars):** "CompTIA A+ Core 1 and Core 2" books on Amazon — very accessible for beginners
- **Jason Dion:** Udemy courses frequently on sale for $15–20 — good balance of theory and practice questions
- **Hands-on practice:** Build or repair a PC, install multiple OSes (Windows, Linux), and practice troubleshooting real hardware whenever possible
- **Performance-based questions (PBQs):** Practice these specifically — they appear first on the exam and can be time-consuming; flag and return if stuck
- Take Core 1 before Core 2 — the hardware foundation makes OS and security concepts easier

## Career Impact
- Qualifies for: Help Desk Technician, Desktop Support Specialist, Field Service Technician, IT Support Analyst, Technical Support Representative
- Recognized by the U.S. Department of Defense under DoD 8570/8140 (IAT Level I when paired with a security cert)
- Many employers list it as a preferred or required qualification for Tier 1/Tier 2 IT support roles
- Average salary for A+-certified professionals: $45,000–$65,000 depending on location and additional skills
- Strong foundation for moving into networking, security, or cloud roles with additional certifications

## How It Compares
- **vs. Google IT Support Certificate:** Google's certificate (via Coursera) is faster and cheaper, and is increasingly recognized by employers, but CompTIA A+ carries more weight in traditional enterprise IT hiring and for DoD-adjacent roles. Consider Google's cert as preparation, then validate with A+.
- **vs. Network+:** Network+ is the natural next step after A+. A+ covers hardware and OS fundamentals; Network+ goes deeper into networking concepts. Most professionals should pursue A+ first unless they already have solid IT support experience.
