# CompTIA Security+

## Overview

CompTIA Security+ is the most widely recognized entry-level cybersecurity certification in the industry. It functions as the baseline credential for security practitioners in the United States and carries mandatory status for a broad range of federal, defense, and government contractor positions. The current version (SY0-701) was released in November 2023 and reflects threat landscapes, hybrid environments, and automation that are central to modern security operations.

The exam consists of up to 90 questions — a mix of multiple choice and performance-based questions (PBQs) — completed within 90 minutes. The passing score is 750 out of 900 points. The exam fee is $404 USD.

## Who Should Get This

Security+ is appropriate for IT professionals making the transition into security, help desk and systems administrators looking to validate security knowledge, and anyone targeting federal, military, or defense contractor positions. It is a prerequisite-by-policy in many organizations before a practitioner can be assigned to security-sensitive duties. Entry-level security analysts, junior SOC staff, and IT auditors starting their security career will find Security+ aligns well with their role requirements.

## Requirements

CompTIA recommends but does not formally require Network+ and two years of IT administration experience with a security focus. The exam has no enforced prerequisites — anyone can register and sit for it. In practice, candidates with little hands-on IT experience struggle with the performance-based questions, so the recommended baseline experience is sound advice rather than a gatekeeping formality.

Renewal requires earning 50 Continuing Education Units (CEUs) over a three-year cycle, or retaking the current version of the exam. CEUs can be earned through training, conferences, and work activities. The CompTIA CertMaster CE online course also satisfies renewal automatically.

## Exam Domains

The SY0-701 exam covers five domains:

1. General Security Concepts (12%)
2. Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Mitigations (22%)
3. Security Architecture (18%)
4. Security Operations (28%)
5. Security Program Management and Oversight (20%)

## Study Tips

Security+ has extensive study material available at every price point. Professor Messer's free video series is the most cited free resource in the community. Pair it with the official CompTIA study guide or Jason Dion's course on Udemy. Performance-based questions require hands-on familiarity with concepts — practice configuring firewalls, interpreting logs, and working with network diagrams rather than only reading.

Allocate 2–3 months for preparation if you have 1–2 years of IT experience. Budget more time if you are coming from a non-technical background. Focus study time heavily on the Security Operations domain (28% of the exam). Take multiple practice exams under timed conditions before sitting for the real thing.

## Career Impact

Security+ is effectively the floor credential for U.S. federal and defense cybersecurity work. It is listed by name in DoD Directive 8570 and its successor 8140 (DCWF) as a required certification for IAT Level II and IAM Level I positions. Federal agencies, military branches, and the major defense contractors (Leidos, SAIC, Booz Allen, General Dynamics IT) list it as a minimum requirement for a wide range of security roles.

In the commercial sector, Security+ consistently appears in entry-level security analyst, SOC analyst, and IT security administrator job postings. It demonstrates baseline security competency and shows commitment to the field. Salary data consistently shows a meaningful premium for Security+-certified professionals over non-certified peers at the entry level.

## How It Compares

Security+ vs. CEH: CEH costs significantly more and is perceived as more advanced, but Security+ is more universally required for federal compliance and broader in coverage for entry-level roles. CEH is better suited once you have experience and are targeting offensive or penetration testing paths.

Security+ vs. SSCP: Security+ is more commonly required by name in job postings. SSCP carries ISC2 brand weight and is more operationally focused. Both are reasonable entry-mid credentials; choice depends on target employer and career path.
