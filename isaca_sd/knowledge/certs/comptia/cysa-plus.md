# CompTIA CySA+ — Cybersecurity Analyst

## Overview

CompTIA CySA+ (CS0-003) is a blue team-focused certification targeting threat detection, analysis, and response. It sits above Security+ in the CompTIA pathway and is aimed at practitioners who work in SOC environments, perform threat hunting, analyze security data, and respond to incidents. The credential is vendor-neutral and validates skills that are directly applicable to security analyst and SOC roles across industries.

The exam consists of up to 85 questions — multiple choice and performance-based — completed within 165 minutes. The passing score is 750 out of 900 points. The exam fee is $404 USD.

## Who Should Get This

CySA+ is designed for security analysts, SOC analysts (Tier 2 and above), threat intelligence analysts, and practitioners who spend their time detecting and responding to attacks rather than performing them. It is the natural progression after Security+ for professionals on a defensive security track. If your work involves SIEM platforms, EDR tools, log analysis, vulnerability management, and incident response, CySA+ validates exactly that skill set.

## Requirements

CompTIA recommends Security+ or Network+ plus a minimum of four years of hands-on IT or security experience before attempting CySA+. There are no enforced prerequisites, but the exam's performance-based questions assume genuine familiarity with analyst workflows. Candidates who have worked in a SOC or similar environment for 2–4 years will find the material maps closely to their daily responsibilities.

Renewal requires 60 CEUs over a three-year cycle. CySA+ renewal also renews Security+, which makes it efficient for candidates who hold both.

## Exam Domains

The CS0-003 exam covers four domains:

1. Security Operations (33%)
2. Vulnerability Management (30%)
3. Incident Response and Management (22%)
4. Reporting and Communication (15%)

## Study Tips

CySA+ requires more analytical thinking than Security+. Rather than memorizing definitions, focus on understanding attacker tactics, detection logic, and the reasoning behind defensive responses. Familiarity with the MITRE ATT&CK framework is valuable — several questions expect you to map behaviors to ATT&CK tactics and techniques.

Hands-on practice with SIEM tools (Splunk, Elastic), packet analysis (Wireshark), and vulnerability scanners (Nessus, OpenVAS) will help significantly with performance-based questions. Jason Dion's CySA+ course and Mike Chapple's study guide are widely used. Plan for 2–4 months of preparation depending on your SOC experience level.

Practice reading and interpreting log outputs, network captures, and vulnerability scan reports. Many performance-based questions present you with realistic data and ask you to identify the threat or recommend the correct response.

## Career Impact

CySA+ is approved under DoD Directive 8570/8140 for CSSP Analyst roles, making it relevant for defense and government contractor positions focused on defensive monitoring. In the commercial sector, it validates the skills needed for SOC Tier 2 analyst, threat analyst, incident responder, and vulnerability management specialist roles.

It is particularly useful for demonstrating readiness for analyst work without the experience bar of CISSP or the brand ambiguity of CEH. Hiring managers at organizations that use CompTIA's framework for tiered security certifications view CySA+ as the validated midpoint between entry-level (Security+) and senior (CASP+) competency.

## How It Compares

CySA+ vs. CEH: CEH leans heavily toward offensive techniques and is often pursued by practitioners who want to demonstrate red team or penetration testing knowledge. CySA+ is explicitly defensive and analytical. For SOC analyst and blue team roles, CySA+ is more directly relevant and commands better recognition from hiring managers in those functions. CEH costs significantly more and has a mixed reputation for hands-on validity. Analysts who work primarily on detection and response should prioritize CySA+; those moving toward offensive work should look at CEH or, better, OSCP.

CySA+ vs. SSCP: Both are mid-tier credentials but with different emphases. SSCP covers a broader range of security operations topics including cryptography and access controls. CySA+ is more narrowly focused on the analyst function — threat detection, incident response, and vulnerability management. Choose based on your role.
